WHAT IS BITCOIN?

Bitcoin (BTC) was created in 2009 as a peer-to-peer electronic cash system. It operates on a blockchain — a public ledger secured by millions of computers worldwide.

Unlike traditional currencies, Bitcoin cannot be printed or inflated. Its hard-capped supply of 21 million makes it the digital equivalent of gold — a hedge against inflation and economic uncertainty.

Today Bitcoin is accepted by millions of merchants worldwide, held by major corporations, and traded by hundreds of millions of people globally.
